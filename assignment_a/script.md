# Assignment A — 1-Minute Video Script
## "Three Runners. One Race. What Does the Data Reveal?"

**Total target time:** ~55 seconds  
**Format:** 3 short talking-head clips intercut with presentation slides  
**Tone:** Energetic, slightly competitive, fun — but academically grounded

---

## SHOOTING PLAN

Film each person separately (phone camera is fine), ideally:
- In running gear OR at DTU
- Outdoors / near campus looks great
- Keep each clip **5–10 seconds max** — tight and punchy
- Film landscape (horizontal), good light on your face

---

## THE SCRIPT

---

### 🎬 SLIDE 1 — Title card (3 sec, auto-play)
*"Three Runners. One Race. What Does the Data Reveal?"*  
*(no voiceover — let it breathe for 2-3 seconds)*

---

### 🎤 ESBEN — ON CAMERA (8 sec)
**[Film: Esben looking confident, maybe in running kit]**

> *"My name is Esben. I train five times a week.  
> I track every run, every heart rate, every split.  
> The Lyngby Half Marathon is not a challenge for me —  
> it's a dataset."*

---

### 🎬 SLIDE 2 — The Cast slide (4 sec)
*(Show runner cards: Esben / Marta / Sergi with their roles)*  
**Voiceover — Marta:**

> *"Three runners. Three training histories. One course."*

---

### 🎤 MARTA — ON CAMERA (8 sec)
**[Film: Marta, energetic, maybe stretching or running shoes visible]**

> *"I'm Marta. I've been training consistently  
> for this — proper long runs, the intervals, everything.  
> We're collecting GPS and heart rate data from all three of us  
> to tell the data story of this race."*

---

### 🎬 SLIDE 3 — The Dataset (5 sec)
*(Pan through stat cards: 61 runs, 510km, 50MB, 98 variables)*  
**Voiceover — Marta:**

> *"We're working with 11 months of Strava data —  
> 61 runs, 510 kilometers, 50 megabytes of GPS files  
> with 98 variables per activity."*

---

### 🎬 SLIDE 4 — EDA slide (4 sec)
*(Show the distribution bar chart and key findings)*  
**Voiceover — Esben:**

> *"Our preliminary analysis shows the training load —  
> distance distributions, heart rate zones,  
> and the key choke point: a 4% gradient at kilometer 14."*

---

### 🎬 SLIDE 5 — Visualization Mockup (5 sec)
*(Show the interactive map mockup)*  
**Voiceover — Marta:**

> *"The final visualization is an interactive course heatmap —  
> the route color-coded by gradient,  
> with each runner's heart rate overlaid at every GPS point."*

---

### 🎬 SLIDE 6 — Genre (3 sec)
*(Genre slide — quick)*  
**Voiceover — Esben:**

> *"We're using the Annotated Chart and Interactive Slideshow genres  
> from Segel and Heer — the map IS the axis,  
> and the story unfolds kilometer by kilometer."*

---

### 🎤 SERGI — ON CAMERA (8 sec)
**[Film: Sergi, slightly sheepish but charming]**

> *"I'm Sergi. I... haven't trained as much as them.  
> But that's exactly why I'm the most scientifically interesting.  
> My heart rate data on that climb at kilometer 14  
> is going to be very dramatic."*

---

### 🎬 SLIDE 7 — Closing (4 sec)
*(Closing slide: "The data knows who trained.")*  
**Voiceover — all three / Marta:**

> *"April 26th. We race. The data speaks.  
> Lyngby Half Marathon — Social Data Analysis, DTU."*

---

## EDITING GUIDE (Canva / CapCut / iMovie)

1. **Start** with Slide 1 (2 sec hold, no audio)
2. **Cut** to Esben clip
3. **Cut** to Slide 2 + Marta voiceover
4. **Cut** to Marta clip
5. **Cut** to Slides 3–4 with voiceovers
6. **Cut** to Slides 5–6 with voiceovers
7. **Cut** to Sergi clip
8. **End** on Slide 7

**Music:** Low-key electronic / running playlist style, ~30% volume under voiceovers  
**Transitions:** Simple cuts — no fade, no wipe. Fast and clean.

---

## CANVA TIPS

1. Upload the PPTX to Canva → it imports each slide automatically
2. Use **Canva Video** → create a new presentation video project
3. Add the talking-head clips as video elements on top of or between slides
4. Record voiceovers directly in Canva (mic icon) or add them as audio files
5. Export as **MP4** at 1080p
